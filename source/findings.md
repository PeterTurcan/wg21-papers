### How The ISO Directives Help

All of the suggestions here lie with the convener's unilateral power. They are presented in order of increasing political difficulty, and reflect a causal chain: First you make people aware they have rights and give them the structural tools to exercise those rights - appeal mechanisms, unrestricted ballot comments, transparent records - so that the sealed room becomes visible and the silent majority discovers it was never actually silent, just uninformed. Then you legitimize AI authorship so that any single engineer with a correct insight can produce documented, cited, adversarially-tested papers at the same throughput as a funded corporate delegation, shattering the resource asymmetry that made the peerage's gatekeeping possible. Then you redesign the institutions - open competition, fixed terms, NB confirmation, incumbent evaluation - so that the people who now have both the information and the capacity to act can channel that action into structural accountability rather than exit.

> * Inform national bodies of their Directive rights.
> * Formal appeal rights at three levels.
> * Establish fitness-for-office criteria for subgroup chairs.
> * Announce open competition for opened chair positions.
> * Unrestricted National Body ballot comments.
> * Advisory groups require committee authorization.

### How Board Meetings Actually Work

> Summarize the effects of adopting this workflow in a single brutal paragraph: technical discussion and design is moved to the mailing list reflectors, which are made public with open participation from delegates and non-delegates. All design work and feedback happens there. Before the face-to-face meetings disputes are attempted to be resolved before hand. When the face-to-face meeting actually happens, there is little to no arguing or technical presentation. There is only the official recording of positions and objections into the record (which itself is also public). Votes are taken in a new way free from the pathologies described above. The meetings become like board meetings: the politicking is done in between the meetings, in public (mostly), and then the face-to-face is merely a procedure with little work from the chair.
